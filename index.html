THE HISTORY OF SBG

The history of the 82-hectare, now UNESCO World Heritage site is long-standing, with more than 150 years of history. Singapore Botanic Gardens’ (SBG) history started all the way back in 1822 and holds a unique and significant place in the history of Singapore and the region. The SBG is the first and only botanic garden in UNESCO’s World Heritage List and is the first in Asia and third botanic garden inscribed in the world.

A BRIEF INTRODUCTION TO THE FOUR ENTRANCES

SBG has four entrances, as it covers so much land and has so many visitors coming to its various gardens on a daily basis. The garden’s versatile, multifunctional nature makes it such a great place to exercise, socialise, dine, pursue hobbies and relax for everyone.

Tanglin Entrance (MAIN)

Entering the Main Gate (also known as Tanglin Gate), visitors will come upon the oldest part of the Gardens, established here at Tanglin in 1859. Some of the key highlights or areas to visit include the Bandstand, Bonsai Garden, Botany Centre, Heritage Museum & CDL Gallery. Swan Lake and Vanda Miss Joaqim.


Tyersall Gallop Entrance

The “Our Rainforest Heritage'' mural at the Tyersall Gate Visitor Centre was designed by artist Eng Siak Loy who also designed the Singapore Botanic Gardens stamp series as well as the Tanglin Gate. It depicts the flora and fauna that can be found in the Gardens’ Rain Forest and the Learning Forest. Key highlights or areas to visit include the Ginger Garden, National Orchid Garden and the Learning Forest.


Nassim Entrance

The Visitor Centre accessible from the Nassim Entrance showcases the best of Southeast Asian architecture and provides a welcome retreat for visitors after a long walk. Must-visit places include the E J H Corner House, the Evolution Garden, the Fragrant Garden, the Healing Garden, the Palm Valley and Symphony Lake as well as the Rain Forest.


Bukit Timah Entrance

The Bukit Timah entrance provides visitors with a plethora of places to visit according to their interests, in a way similar to the other entrances. Specific interest groups may include those who are interested in ecology, families looking for family/child friendly places or individuals who just want to bathe in nature and happen to live around Bukit Timah. Highlights and key places include the Eco-Garden and Eco-Lake, Ethnobotany Garden, Foliage Garden, Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden, the Seed Bank and the Trellis Garden.



RESEARCH FACILITIES

The SBG has five key research facilities including the Herbarium, Library of Botany and Horticulture, Molecular Biology and Micropropagation Lab, the Orchid Breeding and Reintroductions Lab and the Seed Bank. Some of these can be visited, and the opening hours and descriptions are provided below.

Herbarium

The SBG Herbarium (international acronym SING) houses a main collection of about 750,000 herbarium specimens as well as a supporting spirit collection. The Herbarium collections mainly include materials from the Malesian region (Peninsular Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Brunei, Indonesia, the Philippines and New Guinea) and adjacent areas (East Asia, mainland SE Asia, the Southwest Pacific), with the most extensive collections from Singapore and Peninsular Malaysia dating from the 1880s.

Library of Botany and Horticulture

The Library was founded in 1875 in the Singapore Botanic Gardens and is one of the oldest specialized libraries in Southeast Asia. The Library was renamed in 2005 as the Library of Botany and Horticulture.It holds over 120,000 accessioned items, including more than 30,000 books. The library’s collections are held in two areas: The Public Reference Library (unrestricted access & open to public) and the Staff Library (restricted access).

Opening Hours

Public Reference Library: Mon-Fri, 9:00-17:00 (Closed on Weekends)
Staff Library: Restricted

Molecular Biology and Micropropagation

Following renovation in late 2015 the facilities of the Molecular Biology Laboratory have been expanded and improved to keep pace with the increasing importance of molecular data in botany, conservation and horticulture.

Orchid Breeding and Reintroductions

The laboratory has produced many significant orchid hybrids over the course of its history. Orchids are named after foreign dignitaries and celebrities to commemorate their visits to the Gardens. The Gardens has registered over 630 Gardens hybrids since 1932. The laboratory also works on conservation efforts through the ‘Orchid Conservation Programme’ initiated in 1995 to monitor the threatened native species and explore conservation methods. They have reintroduced 33 species to date across Singapore.

Seed Bank

Opened on 13 July 2019, the Singapore Botanic Gardens Seed Bank is a conservation, research and education facility in the Singapore Botanic Gardens that focuses on conserving plant species by preserving the seeds and germplasm of plants in Southeast Asia. The bank also has an interesting gallery that introduces itself and explains its importance in plant conservation and research in Singapore. The Seed Bank also has a seed dispersal garden with seed sculptures showing different seed dispersing mechanisms.

Opening Hours

Seed Bank Interpretive Gallery & Seed Dispersal Garden: Daily from 9:00-18:00. (Closed every last Friday of the month).
